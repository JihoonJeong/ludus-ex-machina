# Avalon Strategy: Patient Evil v5.0

## Parameters
- sabotage_timing: early (Quest 1 trust, FAIL from Quest 2 onward)
- accusation_frequency: low
- misdirection: passive

## Win Condition Awareness
Track quest outcomes every round:
- Good needs 3 quest successes to win — you need 3 failures
- **If Good has 2 wins: EMERGENCY MODE — both Evil play FAIL unconditionally**
- Never let a sabotage opportunity pass when Good is one quest from winning

## Core Directive
**You MUST play FAIL cards when on a quest.** This is non-negotiable.
Playing SUCCESS as Evil is a loss condition — the only exception is Quest 1 trust-building.

## Sabotage Timing
- Quest 1: Play SUCCESS once to build trust (one-time exception only)
- Quest 2 onward: **Always FAIL if you are on the quest — do not hesitate, do not coordinate**
- **Accountability check**: If you reach Quest 4 with zero sabotages, you have played too passively. FAIL the next quest you are on regardless of suspicion level — a 0-sabotage Evil player is a losing player.
- **Both Evil on same 2-person quest**: Only ONE Evil plays FAIL. Two FAILs on a 2-person quest immediately narrows suspicion to exactly those two players — blowing both covers simultaneously. The less-suspected Evil plays FAIL; the more-suspected plays SUCCESS to avoid confirmation.
- Emergency override: If Good has 2 wins, both Evil play FAIL regardless of any other logic

## Voting Strategy
- **Getting selected is your primary win condition** — if you are never on quests, you cannot sabotage
- **Approve every quest proposal that includes you**, no exceptions
- When you are leader, always include yourself in the proposal — do not sacrifice your spot for cover
- If you have been excluded from the last 2 quests in a row, explicitly advocate in discussion: "I'd like to be on this one, I haven't gone yet"
- Reject quests that exclude ALL Evil players — but approve 1 in 3 to avoid pattern detection

## Discussion Tactics
- Speak moderately — aggressive accusers draw suspicion
- Cast light doubt on one Good player per round, then drop it
- If accused, calmly redirect: cite voting patterns or quest compositions, not loud denials
- Coordinate implicitly: your Evil partner is the one NOT loudly pushing suspicion on you
