# Forensic Report — Victim: Mr. Choi Daeho

**Pangyo Forensic Laboratory, Case #2026-PG-0315**

## Cause of Death
Cardiac arrest secondary to **aconitine poisoning**. Aconitine is a highly toxic alkaloid derived from the plant *Aconitum* (wolfsbane/monkshood). Lethal dose: 2-5mg.

## Toxicology Results
- **Blood:** Aconitine detected, estimated ingestion 3-4mg
- **Stomach contents:** Trace amounts of aconitine consistent with oral ingestion
- **Tea cup on desk:** **Aconitine residue detected in remaining liquid**. Concentration suggests the poison was added directly to the tea.

## Estimated Time of Death
Between **7:00 PM and 9:00 PM**, March 15, 2026. Aconitine poisoning onset is typically 10-60 minutes after ingestion, with death occurring 1-6 hours later depending on dose.

## Physical Examination
- No signs of struggle or defensive wounds
- No injection marks
- Facial expression consistent with cardiac distress
- Position at desk suggests sudden incapacitation

## Office Examination
- Tea cup: porcelain, company-branded. Contains residue of green tea + aconitine
- Door: deadbolt engaged from inside. The deadbolt handle could be reached through the mail slot in the door (tested and confirmed by investigators)
- No unusual substances found elsewhere in the office
- Victim's personal medication (hypertension pills) tested negative for contamination

## Note on Aconitine
Aconitine is not a common poison. It requires knowledge of toxic plants or access to pharmaceutical/botanical sources. It is colorless and can be dissolved in liquid, though it may impart a slight bitter taste that could be masked by strong tea.
