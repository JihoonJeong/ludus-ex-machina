# CCTV Summary — Gallery Maru and Vicinity

**Compiled by:** Seoul Metropolitan Police, Jongno District, CCTV Analysis Unit
**Date range:** March 10, 2026, 8:00 PM – March 11, 2026, 2:00 AM

## Gallery Interior Cameras
- **Main exhibition hall:** Camera was disconnected. Gallery owner states it was turned off during the private exhibition "at the request of guests who preferred not to be recorded." This is standard practice for VIP events at Gallery Maru.
- **Owner's office:** No camera installed.
- **Storage room:** No camera installed.

## Gallery Exterior Cameras
- **Front entrance camera:** Operational. Recorded all guests arriving and departing. No one entered through the front entrance after 10:30 PM (when Mr. Lee locked up).
- **Rear exit camera:** **Non-operational.** Gallery owner states this camera has been broken for approximately 2 weeks. A repair request was filed on February 28 but not yet completed. **This fact was mentioned during the exhibition — several guests overheard Mr. Lee complaining about the broken camera to a staff member.**

## Street CCTV (Samcheong-dong area)
- Camera at intersection of Samcheong-ro and Bukchon-ro:
  - 23:35 — Black sedan heading north (toward Gallery Maru area)
  - 23:55 — Same or similar black sedan heading south (away from Gallery Maru)
  - License plate not readable due to camera angle
- No pedestrians recorded in the rear alley (no camera coverage in alley)

## Key Finding
The thief used the rear exit — the only unwatched entry/exit point. The broken rear camera was common knowledge among exhibition attendees. Anyone present at the exhibition would have known that the rear exit was unmonitored.
