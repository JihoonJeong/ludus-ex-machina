# Case Brief: The Midnight Collector

## Incident Summary

On the night of March 10, 2026, the painting *Moonrise Over Bukhansan* by the late artist Seo Youngho was stolen from Gallery Maru in Samcheong-dong, Seoul. The painting, valued at approximately ₩800 million, was the centerpiece of a private exhibition that evening.

The gallery owner, Mr. Lee Sangwoo, locked up at 10:30 PM after the last guest departed. When he returned at 8:00 AM the next morning, the painting was gone. The alarm system had been disabled, and the rear emergency exit showed signs of use.

## Initial Assessment

No signs of forced entry at any door or window. The alarm was disabled electronically, not bypassed. This suggests the thief had access to the alarm system or obtained the master keycard. The painting was cut from its frame — a method used by professionals who intend to sell through underground channels.

## Suspects

- **Suspect A (Ms. Han Jiyeon):** Gallery assistant. Has worked at Gallery Maru for 2 years. Knows the alarm system. Was present at the exhibition until 9:00 PM.

- **Suspect B (Mr. Shin Wonjae):** Independent art dealer. Attended the exhibition as a guest. Was seen in a heated argument with the gallery owner about a commission dispute earlier that week.

- **Suspect C (Dr. Park Minsoo):** Private art collector. Lost a bidding war for the same painting at auction 6 months ago. Attended the exhibition and was the last guest to leave at 10:15 PM.
