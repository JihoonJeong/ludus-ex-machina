# Financial Dispute — Jung Taeyang & Kim Hyunwoo

**Source:** Documents provided by Mr. Kim Hyunwoo and independently verified

## Background

In January 2025, Mr. Kim Hyunwoo (through Mirae Asset Securities) arranged for three private investors to commit ₩3B to a real estate development project in Wirye New Town, managed by the victim's company, Hangang Development Corp. Mr. Kim personally guaranteed the investment to his clients based on his friendship with the victim.

## Timeline of Dispute

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Jan 2025 | Investment of ₩3B committed. Projected completion: Dec 2025. |
| Jun 2025 | Project delayed — permitting issues. New target: Mar 2026. |
| Sep 2025 | Second delay — construction cost overruns. Mr. Kim begins receiving complaints from investors. |
| Dec 2025 | Original completion deadline passes. No progress update from victim. |
| Jan 2026 | Mr. Kim requests formal project status report. Victim provides vague assurances. |
| Feb 2026 | One investor threatens legal action against Mr. Kim personally. |
| Mar 13, 2026 | Mr. Kim sends formal written demand via lawyer (Kim & Associates): return ₩3B within 30 days or face arbitration. |
| Mar 15, 2026 | Argument at reunion (day of death). |

## Financial Exposure

- Mr. Kim's personal liability (guarantee): Up to ₩3B
- Mr. Kim's annual income: ~₩400M (salary + bonus)
- If investors sue, Mr. Kim could face personal bankruptcy
- Mr. Kim's lawyer (Kim & Associates) confirmed the demand letter was sent March 13

## Motive Assessment

Mr. Kim had a strong financial grievance against the victim. However:
- The victim's death does **not** resolve Mr. Kim's financial problem — the ₩3B is still tied up in the project.
- Hangang Development Corp.'s board would need to decide on the project's future. The victim's death could actually **complicate** fund recovery, not simplify it.
- Mr. Kim's stated strategy (legal arbitration) was already in motion and had reasonable prospects.
- **Killing the victim would make fund recovery harder, not easier.**

**Assessment: Strong emotional motive (anger, betrayal of trust), but weak rational motive. The victim's death is financially counterproductive for Mr. Kim.**
