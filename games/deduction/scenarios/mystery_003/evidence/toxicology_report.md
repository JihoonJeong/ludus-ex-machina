# Toxicology Report — Victim: Mr. Jung Taeyang

**National Forensic Service, Toxicology Division**
**Case Reference: 2026-GP-0322-TOX**

## Summary

In addition to ethanol (BAC 0.12%), the following substance was detected:

**Midazolam** — a benzodiazepine class sedative/hypnotic
- Blood concentration: **180 ng/mL** (therapeutic range: 40-100 ng/mL; sedation threshold: 80-150 ng/mL)
- Estimated dose ingested: **15-20mg** (standard medical dose: 7.5-15mg)
- The concentration detected is **above the sedation threshold** and would cause significant drowsiness, impaired coordination, and potential loss of consciousness — especially in combination with alcohol (BAC 0.12%).

## Interaction Analysis

Midazolam + alcohol is a well-documented dangerous combination:
- Both are CNS depressants
- Effects are **synergistic**, not merely additive
- At the detected levels, the combined effect would likely cause:
  - Profound sedation / loss of consciousness within 20-40 minutes of ingestion
  - Respiratory depression
  - Loss of protective reflexes (including the ability to keep one's head above water)

## Source Analysis

- Midazolam is a **prescription medication** (Schedule IV controlled substance in Korea)
- Available as oral tablets (7.5mg, 15mg) or injectable solution
- **Not available over the counter.** Requires a prescription or access through medical/pharmaceutical channels.
- The oral form can be crushed and dissolved in liquid. It is **nearly tasteless** in small quantities, though may impart a very slight bitterness that would be masked by alcohol.

## Residue Testing

Crime scene investigators collected all glasses and bottles from the pension.
- **Victim's whiskey glass** (identified by fingerprints, found on hot tub deck): **Midazolam residue detected.**
- All other glasses: Negative for midazolam.
- Whiskey bottle (Yamazaki 18, shared among group): Negative for midazolam.

## Conclusion

Midazolam was introduced into the victim's individual glass, not into the shared bottle. This indicates **targeted administration** rather than accidental contamination. The victim was selectively drugged.
